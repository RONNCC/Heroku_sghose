National Youth Advisory Council Member

            11, 12

Though I haven't been doing this as long as a few of my other activities, it ranks high among my favorite easily. To me, being a YAC member means planning out all of the Botball tournaments and thinking how can we help to make them work better and more accessible! It means, me standing on a chair awkwardly setting up cameras in front of an audience of a few hundred people at the International Botball Conference - GCER. Those cameras, which I later connected using RCA cables, were the backbone of the web streaming service I and YAC runs, though I was responsible for it at GCER. In addition, I set up the electronics for around 60 tables, and taped all of it down. We went through around 6 rolls of gaffers tape, and I got some exercise running around! It means looking at a giant library of code, which one of the VPs and I am working on, in order to make the upcoming regionals function better and more accurately start the robots. It also means I'll be running for President in January 2013!

Future Problem Solvers

            10, 11, 12

                This club is one in which I did not have formally a ‘position’ role, and sadly could not run for one this year due to mentorship which does not allow it :(. However that did not in the slightest mean that I could not help out! Last year it was around the beginning of the year when my team submitted our first fuzzy - or Future Problem Situation - solution. It was on a future ecological problem, pollution in the coral reef. We thought the main issue of a program that aimed to send a scientist year round to test the coral reef was that he himself would disturb the environment and cause for pollution. Soon later after another problem set we reached states! I looked at the topic and loved it, it was economics. I asked if anyone wanted a copy of a vocab sheet I was going to make as I wanted to look up some ideas and terms in preparation. This then was expanded to become a club wide project to make a study guide for States! At States one of our ideas was to bring back Gandhi to help organize the Italian economy, stagnated in our future scene. When we came back, I helped to modify the constitution and try to organize a middle school invitational and outreach!

 

FIRST Robotics Club

                11, 12

                So quickly some background, when I joined, the team only allowed Juniors and Seniors after they took the robotics or prototyping courses. I was confused why, many other teams allowed any interested individual to participate and actually encouraged underclassmen since they would be able to accumulate experience better as they would stay longer. As a result of my lobbying, and soon my teammates, we now allow sophmores! :) . It's not everyone, but it's a start. As a junior I was the assistant lead of programming and electronics - two separate 'teams', or so I would say. In reality it was one senior for electronics and another for programming with one 'understudy' in both - me. This meant that I striped wires and checked readings for electronics and then switched to develop computer vision algorithms using the National Instruments Library. I had fun doing it though, arriving early every Saturday and staying till the latest I could! This year, I'm part of electronics and programming again, but they now have three or so people per team. My role is more of an accessory one for now, helping fix some bugs until the season starts, at which point I'll be wiring and coding again! Regretfully I still won't be able to be the leads of both teams due to mentorship, which doesn't allow one to attend club periods, so I will be an assistant lead again!

TJ Volunteering / Volunteering

            9, 10, 11, 12

My favorite volunteering to talk about though is the volunteering I do at TJ - the popular abbreviation for my high school. So the volunteering I do there is the only time I can say I skip around doing math, which I think is the most fun thing to say! I mean who else can say they had fun with math AND while teaching middle schoolers! In this case it was teaching them proofs and AMC style problems for TJIMO - TJ's Math Invitational for Middle Schoolers. Similarly I volunteered for TJ's Techstravaganza where I taught water cycles and the aerodynamics of a paper plane to middle schoolers! And in TJ Spontaneous, I organized the event so that the 3 hour delay, suddenly disappeared ... which actually made me part of the organizing committee this year ironically. So I think though I can volunteer at other places and do quite frequently, my favorite place to volunteer is at TJ because I get to teach other students and middle schoolers :)! Also math is supposed to be fun, I think skipping around doing math is the closest you can get (something my team did during TJIMO)!
